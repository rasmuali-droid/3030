Kickboxing rules allow punches and kicks (above the waist or leg kicks, depending on style) to the body and head, generally prohibiting elbows, throws, and groin strikes. Bouts consist of 3–10 rounds, typically 2–3 minutes each, using the 10-point must system. Common rulesets include K-1 (limited clinch), Full Contact (no leg kicks), and Oriental.

Key Rules and Regulations
Allowed Techniques: Punches, kicks to the head/body/legs, and knee strikes (allowed in some formats like K-1, but prohibited in traditional American rules).
Forbidden Techniques: Elbow strikes, headbutts, groin strikes, biting, and spinning back fist with the glove.
Clinching: Generally, extended clinching is prohibited. Some rules allow a 5-second window for knee strikes, while others forbid it entirely.
Match Duration: Amateur bouts typically last 3 rounds, while professional matches can go up to 10 rounds, with 1-minute rest periods.
Scoring: Matches are scored on a 10-point must system, evaluating knockdowns, damage, clean strikes, and aggression.
Knockdowns: A fighter is considered down if any part of the body other than the feet touches the floor, or if they are "rocked" and unable to properly defend themselves.
Equipment: Required gear includes boxing gloves, mouthguard, groin guard, and hand wraps.

Common Discipline Differences
K-1 Rules: Allows knee strikes and limited clinching.
Full Contact: Prohibits leg kicks and sometimes limits, or forbids, clinching.
Low Kick (Oriental): Allows leg kicks.

Fouls and Penalties
Common penalties include, but are not limited to, holding the leg without attacking, sweeping below the knee, and hitting after the referee breaks the fighters.
