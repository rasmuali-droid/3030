While offering connectivity, it is heavily linked to increased anxiety, depression, depression, low self-esteem, sleep disruption, and cyberbullying. Key Negative Effects
Mental Health Issues: High usage is associated with increased anxiety, depression, and psychological distress.
Addiction and Compulsion: Features like "likes" trigger dopamine, leading to addictive, slot-machine-like usage patterns.
Body Image and Self-Esteem: Constant exposure to curated, idealized, or filtered images leads to body dissatisfaction and reduced self-worth, particularly in teenagers.
Cyberbullying and Harassment: A significant portion of users experience online abuse, causing severe, lasting emotional harm.
Sleep Disruption: Nighttime scrolling disrupts sleep cycles, affecting overall health.
Fear of Missing Out (FOMO): Constant checking creates anxiety over missing events or updates, leading to social media dependency.
Cognitive and Behavioral Issues: Excessive use is linked to attention deficits, reduced physical activity, and decreased, say the Stanford Law School and The Annie E. Casey Foundation.
