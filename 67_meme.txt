The "6-7" (or "67") meme is a popular, largely nonsensical, and viral "brainrot" slang term that originated on TikTok and Instagram Reels in early 2025. It is primarily used by Generation Alpha and younger Gen Z to signify a "vibe," a "so-so" status, or simply to be intentionally absurd.

Origins and Key Elements
The Song: The meme comes from the song "Doot Doot (6 7)" by rapper Skrilla, released in 2024, which features the repeated hook "six-seven".
LaMelo Ball: The audio quickly became associated with TikTok edits of NBA player LaMelo Ball, who is 6 feet, 7 inches tall.
The "6-7 Kid": In March 2025, a viral video of a boy at an Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) basketball game shouting "six-seven" while performing a specific hand gesture (alternating hands up and down with palms up) turned him into a viral sensation known as the "67 Kid".
"67" vs. "Sixty-Seven": The term is always pronounced "six-seven" and never "sixty-seven".

Context and Usage
Meaning: It has no fixed definition and is purposefully nonsensical, serving as a form of "brainrot" humor. It is used as a catch-all phrase to mean "so-so," "maybe," or just to express a chaotic vibe.
Context: It is often shouted in classrooms, causing disruption, and is frequently accompanied by the hand gesture.
Reactions: It has become so prevalent that it has been described as "annoying" and "like a plague". Some schools have struggled to manage the trend.
Cultural Impact: In late 2025, Google introduced an Easter egg where searching for "6-7" causes the screen to shake.
